How does this work?

When you sign up for Swap Drop Enroll, our program holds on to your login information and waits for a spot to open up in your desired course. The moment this happens, our program automatically logs you in and registers you faster than greased lightning.



Why should I trust you with my onyen?

Your ONYEN information is stored in a secure, encrypted database that no one is able to view (not even us). Basically, we use the power of math to make sure that no one can access our database. You can find out more here: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Encryption



Me and three friends are all trying to get into COMP 110. When a spot opens up in the class, who do you register first?

Excellent question! If more than one ONYEN is signed up to register for the same class, we put all of the ONYENs into an electronic hat, and we pick one of these ONYENs at random. This is how we keep Swap Drop Enroll equitable for our users.



Is there any way that I can increase my chances of getting picked for a popular class?

Wow, another amazing question! In fact, this is possible. You can increase your chances of being picked if you refer your friends. By referring your friend, your ONYEN is entered again in the electronic hat. If a friend refers you, your name is also entered an additional time in the hat.
